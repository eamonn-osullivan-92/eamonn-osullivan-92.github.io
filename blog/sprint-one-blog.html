Identity, Values and Strengths

A self-reflection

28 June 2022

1. Describe your core personal values.

Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and the consequences of your action.

Prior to leaving my previous job in the Travel Industry, the company was going through a rough time with a lot of staff turnover. While things were certainly starting to improve, there weren't a lot of staff who knew how the business worked outside of a COVID environment. I knew that leaving would put the company, and particularly my manager, in a tough spot as I was the only one left who knew how parts of the business functioned. Surviving the COVID period in the travel industry meant that everyone had to 'wear many hats' and take on multiple roles, so I knew that there wasn;'t anyone at the company that could comfortably take on my role.

I was grateful for the support and belief the company had shown in me during my employment, so I decided to be upfront about my decision to leave the company as soon as I had made the decision (approx. 6 months before I was due to move back to New Zealand). I did this because I knew that there would need to be new staff hired, trained and I was one of the few that could do it.

I have always been quite an independent worker. I like to have a well defined role that I can execute on my own, so the training and leadership role I took on was definitely out of my comfort zone. The extra workload that came with it was also a factor, as I was already feeling the heat with my current role, but I am thankful I committed to it. It was a great growth opportunity for me, and it was reassuring to be able to provide knowledge and expertise to newer staff members. It definitely helped give me confidence in my own abilities, possibly more than I had previously given myself credit for.

Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

Two Maori proverbs I was taught in school and have stuck with me since:

Tangata ako ana i te whare, te turanga ki te marae, tau ana; A person who mistreats his guest has a dusty Marae (Meeting house). This encapsulates life as a whole. Treat others with kindness, compassion, have a positive and encouraging outlook and your life will be richer for it.

Kāore te kumara e kōrero mō tōna ake reka. The kumara (sweet potato) does not say how sweet he is. I always loved the sound of this one. It reminds me to be humble and grateful for the life and opportunities I have.

2. Describe your strengths and limitations.

Evaluate your strengths and limitations in terms of your learning and career development

Strengths:

  • I have a natural curiosity to want to understand how things work
  • I am Self-sufficient
  • I can organize & manage a busy workload well
  • I have a cool/calm temperament

Limitations:

  • I can be reserved & independent
  • I am not a natural leader
  • I lack confidence to back my own ideas and answers in a group environment

Identify which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey and how they intersect with learning obstacles.

A natural curiosity to want to understand how things work:
Since beginning to learn programming, this curiosity has helped me a lot. Oftentimes I have been stuck on a particular problem or obstacle, and through extensive google searching or trial and error you find a way to make it work. But this doesn't necessarily mean you fully understand the concept or issue that you were faced with, so I will always dig deeper and find out the why's and how's of the problem. How did the problem arise in the first place? What is the solution actually doing in the grand scheme? Could the problem be avoided (is the solution necessary). These sorts of critical questions have helped me immensely since beginning to program, and in other learning environments.

In the context of my learning obsticles, being more comfortable with a particular concept or topic helps overcome any confidence issues i might have, making me a more outspoken and outgoing participant in a learning or professional environment.

Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

A previous job of mine was to stress test and audit travel packages that would be submitted by colleagues. A lot of this involved providing suggestions on how a travel package could be improved, be it price related or product related. The colleagues that created the travel packages were not always as welcoming and open to criticism as you would like (speaking from personal experience, as I have worked in both roles), so there was often resistance and tension.

I found it was always important to get a deeper understanding of where the tensions were coming from. Do they not agree with my criticisms or suggestions? Or is it the stress of deadlines and workload that is making them uncomfortable with making late changes. I would always make sure to break down my criticisms from the perspective of a customer, and how it might affect either their experience, or their desire to purchase the product. Another strategy was to not just say what I thought was wrong, but what I think would be better. Even providing a few different options that I thought would improve the product and opening a discussion to find something we both agree on. This would speed up the process for the product creator to confirm any changes with their overseas partners and ease the workload for all involved.

For now and the future, I'm going to focus on making a real effort to understand the person. Problems generally arise from a clash of personalities, not opinions. I don’t consider myself too proud or anything, I know I'm not always right. The best solution is one that both parties agree on, so understanding who the other party is, what their values are, and why they feel the way they do will go a long way to creating a better work environment.
